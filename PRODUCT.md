# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

London local business owners — hair salons, restaurants, tradespeople, retailers, beauty therapists, gyms. Not technical. Typically searching on mobile after being let down by a cheap template or having no website at all. They want to look credible online without dealing with a big agency, a long timeline, or an unpredictable bill.

## Product Purpose

RB Studio's own marketing and portfolio site. It's the studio's primary sales pitch to local businesses. Success means an enquiry form submission. The site must demonstrate — not just describe — what good web design looks like, because every design decision is simultaneously a portfolio piece.

## Brand Personality

Clean, confident, local. Professional and trustworthy, but approachable. A real London studio with a human behind it, not a faceless agency with corporate language.

## Anti-references

- **Big agency vibes** — corporate, cold, buzzword-heavy, feels like a 50-person firm
- **Generic Wix/Squarespace look** — template aesthetic, stock photos, cookie-cutter layouts indistinguishable from the clients it serves
- **Cheap/low-budget feel** — anything that looks like it was built for free undermines the credibility of a paid design service

## Design Principles

1. **Practice what you preach.** The studio's own site is the loudest statement about what it sells. Every font choice, spacing decision, and interaction is a live demo.
2. **Local, not small.** Warmth and neighbourhood proximity matter, but the work must look as sharp as any London agency output.
3. **Clarity earns trust.** Transparent pricing, honest copy, no jargon. Busy business owners want to know exactly what they're getting.
4. **Show, don't sell.** Let portfolio and testimonials do the convincing. Minimise marketing copy; maximise credibility signals.
5. **Restraint over decoration.** Every visual element earns its place. Clutter signals a designer who hasn't made the hard choices.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Proper contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, semantic HTML, screen reader support. Mobile-first — the primary audience is likely on a phone.
